Mogudo/Tripe/Matumbu

Description

Mogodu is a traditional South African dish made from beef tripe (the lining of a cow’s stomach). It’s usually slow-cooked until tender and enjoyed with pap (maize meal porridge). It’s hearty, rich in flavor, and often considered comfort food.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg beef tripe (cleaned and cut into pieces)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped or blended)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1–2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 tsp curry powder or paprika (optional, for flavor)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Water or beef stock (enough for boiling)

Instructions

  1. Wash the tripe thoroughly. Place it in a pot, cover with water, and boil until soft (about 2–3 hours). Top up water as needed.
  2. In a separate pan, heat oil and sauté onion and garlic until golden. Add tomatoes and spices, cook into a sauce.
  3. Add boiled tripe to the sauce, season with salt and pepper, and simmer for another 15–20 minutes so flavors blend.
  4. Serve hot with pap, rice, or dumplings.
