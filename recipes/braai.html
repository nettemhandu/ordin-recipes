Pap, Chakalaka and Wors.

Pap

Description

A traditional South African meal consisting of pap (maize porridge), chakalaka (spicy vegetable relish), and wors (boerewors sausage). This hearty dish is often enjoyed at braais (barbecues) and family gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups maize meal
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Instructions

  1. Bring water and salt to a boil in a pot.
  2. Slowly add maize meal while stirring to avoid lumps.
  3. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally until thick and cooked through.
  4. Serve hot with chakalaka, wors, or mogodu.

Chakalaka

Description

Chakalaka is a spicy South African vegetable relish made with beans, peppers, and carrots. It’s often served as a side dish with pap and meat.

Ingredients

  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped or blended)
  • 1 green pepper (chopped)
  • 2 carrots
  • 1 can baked beans
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1–2 tsp curry powder
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan and fry onion, garlic, and green pepper until soft.
  2. Add curry powder and cook for 1–2 minutes.
  3. Stir in tomatoes and carrots, cooking until tender.
  4. Add baked beans, season with salt and pepper, and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Serve warm with pap and wors.

Wors

Description

Wors (short for boerewors) is a traditional South African sausage made with beef, sometimes mixed with pork or lamb, and spiced with coriander and cloves. It’s usually grilled on a braai.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg boerewors sausage
  • Cooking oil or non-stick spray (optional, for frying)

Instructions

  1. If braaing: Place wors on medium-hot coals and cook, turning often, until evenly browned and cooked through (about 15–20 minutes).
  2. If pan-frying: Heat a little oil in a pan, cook wors on medium heat, turning occasionally, until done.
  3. Do not cut wors while cooking; keep it in a coil to retain juices.
  4. Serve with pap and chakalaka.
